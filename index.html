Neonatal Sedation & Analgesia

Intubated Neonates < 30 Days · Pre- & Post-Cardiac Surgery

Inclusion Criteria

  • Intubated neonates < 30 days of age
  • Pre-cardiac surgery

Exclusion Criteria

  • Continuous neuromuscular blockade (paralyzed)
  • ECMO
  • End of life care
  • Pre-existing tolerance requiring individualized plan

Assessment

Assess SBS & rFLACC every 4 hours. If inadequate sedation or pain control, begin escalation below.

RN Directed Step 1

Bolus Morphine

Administer a single dose of morphine 0.025 mg/kg.

Wait 30 min — observe for effectiveness of morphine.

If still inadequate
RN Directed Step 2

Bolus Morphine + IV Tylenol

Administer another morphine dose 0.025 mg/kg + IV Acetaminophen 10 mg/kg.

Wait 30 min — observe for effectiveness of combined treatment.

If still inadequate
Notify Provider Step 3

Increase Dexmedetomidine

Adjust the dexmedetomidine infusion rate up to 0.5 mcg/kg/hr.

Wait 30 min — observe for effectiveness of dexmedetomidine.

If still inadequate
Provider Step 4

Start Morphine Drip

Initiate a continuous morphine infusion at 0.02 mg/kg/hr.

If still inadequate
Provider Step 5

Repeat Morphine Bolus up to 3 Times

Administer additional morphine boluses 0.025 mg/kg if needed.

Reassess every 30 min — evaluate pain levels frequently between each bolus.

If still inadequate
Provider Step 6

Increase Morphine Drip

Increase by +0.01 mg/kg/hr increments.

⚑ No ketamine or midazolam in the preoperative phase.

Assessment & Escalation Triggers

Assess SBS & rFLACC every 4 hours. If SBS or pain score inadequate, begin escalation below.

> 3 PRNs in 6h → increase drip    > 6 PRNs in 12h → further increase

Non-procedural PRNs only. Do not count procedural sedation doses.

RN Directed Step 1

Administer Matching Opioid Bolus up to 3× q30min

Bolus dose equals the current hourly infusion rate. Reassess every 30 min between doses, up to 3 boluses total.

Reassess patient — if still inadequate
Notify Provider Step 2

Notify First-Line Provider

Inform the first-line provider of inadequate pain management despite bolus dosing.

Provider directs drip increase
Provider Step 3

Increase Drip — First Escalation

Increase drip incrementally based on assessment scores:

If rFLACC inadequate
Morphine +0.01 mg/kg/hr
Fentanyl +0.5 mcg/kg/hr
If SBS inadequate
Dex +0.1 mcg/kg/hr
Reassess patient — if still inadequate
Provider Step 4

Increase Drip — Second Escalation

Repeat the same incremental increase:

If rFLACC inadequate
Morphine +0.01 mg/kg/hr
Fentanyl +0.5 mcg/kg/hr
If SBS inadequate
Dex +0.1 mcg/kg/hr
Reassess patient — if still inadequate
Provider Step 5

Midazolam (Versed)

Intermittent: 0.025 mg/kg Q4H PRN

If still inadequate
Provider Step 6

Ketamine

  • Intermittent: 0.5 mg/kg Q4H PRN
  • Infusion: 0.1 mg/kg/hr start, titrate to max 1 mg/kg/hr
Opioid Rotation Guidelines
!
Rotate opioids if inadequate SBS / rFLACC on max dose of current agent.
⚑
When rotating opioids, reduce calculated equianalgesic dose by 25–50% for incomplete cross-tolerance.

IV → Enteral Bridging: Do not start enteral agent until first step of IV agent wean is anticipated within 12 hours of first enteral dose.
Dex → Clonidine

Start when: Dex exposure ≥ 7 days  and  Dex gtt < 1 mcg/kg/hr

Wean target: Dex off within 48–72 hours of starting clonidine.

If unlikely to wean in that window: Discontinue clonidine and continue dex gtt wean alone. Clonidine is not always necessary. Dex-only wean can also be completed on the floor.

Clonidine ≠ Dex. 8× lower α-2 selectivity. Increasing the dose will not replicate dex's sedation profile. Use within dosing range as a weaning bridge only.
Opioid → Morphine / Methadone

Start when: Opioid exposure ≥ 5 days  and  Morphine gtt < 0.15 mg/kg/hr  or  Fentanyl gtt < 2 mcg/kg/hr
