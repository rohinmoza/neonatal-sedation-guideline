Neonatal Sedation & Analgesia

Intubated Neonates < 30 Days

Inclusion Criteria

  • Intubated neonates < 30 days of age
  • Pre-cardiac surgery
  • Non-surgical patients

Exclusion Criteria

  • Continuous neuromuscular blockade (paralyzed)
  • ECMO
  • End of life care
  • Pre-existing tolerance requiring individualized plan

Assessment & Drip Adjustment Rules

Assess sedation and pain scores every 4 hours. Reassess 30 min after any intervention. Review PRN counts every morning on rounds and after resolving any escalation.

> 3 non-procedural PRNs in 6h or > 6 in 12h → increase drip per increments below:

When to Begin Weaning

No non-procedural PRNs given in the prior 12 hours with stable hemodynamics and adequate sedation/pain scores.

Wean Protocol

If on continuous dexmedetomidine only (no opioid drip): Wean dexmedetomidine first. Reduce by 10% of current rate. Monitor for unexplained hypertension or tachycardia.

If on both continuous opioid and dexmedetomidine: Reduce opioid by 10% of current rate. Monitor for withdrawal signs. Wean dexmedetomidine after opioid wean is underway.

Precision when writing wean rates: Fentanyl to 1 decimal place (e.g. 1.2 mcg/kg/hr), morphine to 2 decimal places (e.g. 0.02 mg/kg/hr), dexmedetomidine to 1 decimal place (e.g. 0.4 mcg/kg/hr).

Returning from the OR

Most patients arrive on a fentanyl drip from the OR. Two steps:

1. Bring fentanyl down to 0.5 mcg/kg/hr

2. Order morphine drip to start at 0.02 mg/kg/hr; switch when it arrives from pharmacy

As that transition is happening, also bring dexmedetomidine down to 0.2 mcg/kg/hr — aim to get there within the hour.

Struggling with sedation? Consider Gabapentin (adjunct)

Adjunct for refractory agitation or visceral/neuropathic pain. Reduces opioid and benzodiazepine requirements; not a primary sedative.

Titration (2 to 3 days at each step):

  • 5 mg/kg BID → 5 mg/kg TID → 7.5 mg/kg TID → 10 mg/kg TID (max 30 mg/kg/day)

⚑ Off-label in neonates. Renally cleared; adjust for immature renal function. Wean over ≥1 week.

Opioid Rotation Guidelines
!
Rotate opioids if inadequate sedation / pain scores on max dose of current agent.
⚑
When rotating opioids, reduce calculated equianalgesic dose by 25–50% for incomplete cross-tolerance.

Only start enteral bridge if you expect the IV drip off within 48–72 hours. After tolerating 2–3 enteral doses, begin stepwise drip reduction. Not all patients on dexmedetomidine need clonidine; many can wean dexmedetomidine alone.

At transfer, explicitly communicate all sedative wean plans to the floor team.

Dexmedetomidine → Clonidine

Start when: Dexmedetomidine exposure ≥ 7 days and dexmedetomidine gtt < 1 mcg/kg/hr

Do not start clonidine if dexmedetomidine is at or above 1 mcg/kg/hr. Wean dexmedetomidine below this threshold first.

Not tolerating? Stop clonidine and continue dexmedetomidine-only wean.

Clonidine ≠ Dexmedetomidine. Clonidine has 8× lower α-2 selectivity than dexmedetomidine. Dose escalation will not replicate dexmedetomidine. Weaning bridge only.
Opioid → Morphine / Methadone

Start when: Opioid exposure ≥ 5 days and Morphine gtt < 0.15 mg/kg/hr or Fentanyl gtt < 2 mcg/kg/hr
